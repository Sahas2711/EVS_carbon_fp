What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions. The average carbon footprint for a person in the United States is 16 tons, one of the highest rates in the world.

Globally, the average is closer to 4 tons. To have the best chance of avoiding a 2â„ƒ rise in global temperatures, the average global carbon footprint per year needs to drop to under 2 tons by 2050.

Why It Matters

Climate Change

Carbon emissions are the primary driver of global climate change, leading to extreme weather, rising sea levels, and ecosystem disruption.

Health Impacts

Air pollution from fossil fuels causes millions of premature deaths worldwide each year.

Economic Costs

The effects of climate change could cost the global economy over $500 billion per year by 2050.
